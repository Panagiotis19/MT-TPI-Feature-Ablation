Key Findings

What helps performance
  • Removing orientation_y, wind_angle, and angular_z together improves both accuracy (+0.61%) and trajectory MAE (−0.0001), while reducing the feature set by 15%.
  • Five features are consistently safe to remove across ranking tables: orientation_y, wind_angle, angular_z, linear_acceleration_y, and velocity_z.
  • Dropping position_y alone gives the single largest accuracy gain (+1.44% → 79.81%), but at the cost of a slightly higher MAE.
  • The best 2-feature pair (orientation_z, orientation_y) achieves the lowest MAE in the entire study (0.0034), suggesting orientation features are redundant for trajectory prediction.
What hurts performance
  • position_x, position_z, and battery_current are critical; their removal degrades both classification and trajectory metrics.
  • The TURN class is the most sensitive to feature removal: baseline 50.9% drops to 33.5% when both orientation_y and orientation_z are removed together.
  • Removing 4 features consistently costs ~1–2% accuracy and rarely improves MAE, indicating the architecture has reached its feature-reduction ceiling.
  • Combinations including orientation_x with orientation_w or angular_z degrade TURN recognition sharply (often below 30%).

Surprising Discoveries

  • Less is more: Six single-feature removals and several multi-feature combinations beat the baseline on accuracy, showing that the full 20-feature input is not strictly optimal and the model can exploit a cleaner feature set.
  • Context dependence of orientation features: Removing orientation_x alone drops accuracy by −4.17%, but combined with wind_angle and linear_acceleration_y it contributes to a near-baseline 4-feature configuration. The effect of a feature depends strongly on which others are removed with it.
  • Wind matters less than expected: Both wind_speed and wind_angle rank among the safest features to remove despite the dataset being collected under variable outdoor weather (6–35 km/h winds), suggesting the IMU/velocity channels already capture wind-induced motion implicitly.
  • Trajectory MAE and classification accuracy are only loosely coupled: the configuration with the best MAE (orientation_z, orientation_y, 0.0034) is simultaneously the worst 2-feature configuration for classification accuracy (−3.41%). Optimizing for one metric does not automatically optimize the other.
  • DESCEND is more robust than TURN: contrary to prior expectations, DESCEND accuracy remains largely stable (±3%) across most removals, while TURN swings widely (27–56%). TURN is the true bottleneck class, not DESCEND.
